# Getting Started with Edify OS

Welcome. You just hired a team.

Not software. Not a chatbot. A real team of seven AI specialists -- each one trained to handle a specific part of running a nonprofit. They know fundraising, communications, finance, programs, HR, events, and operations. And they're ready to go right now.

Here's how to get started.

---

## What Is Edify OS?

Edify OS is a management platform built for nonprofit leaders. Instead of juggling a dozen tools, you work with a single team of AI specialists who know your organization, remember your history, and get smarter over time.

Think of it like having a fully-staffed back office -- without the payroll.

---

## Meet Your Team

You have seven specialists on staff from day one:

- **Development Director** -- Grants, donor relationships, fundraising strategy
- **Marketing Director** -- Social media, email campaigns, brand voice, press
- **Executive Assistant** -- Scheduling, email triage, meeting prep, task tracking
- **Programs Director** -- Program design, outcomes, compliance, evaluation
- **Finance Director** -- Budgets, cash flow, audit prep, financial reports
- **HR & Volunteer Coordinator** -- Hiring, volunteers, policies, staff training
- **Events Director** -- Event planning, run-of-show, sponsorships, post-event review

Each specialist has their own expertise. When you need help with fundraising, you go to your Development Director. When you need a social post, you go to Marketing. Simple as that.

---

## Getting Started in 3 Steps

### Step 1: Set Up Your Organization

Before your team can help you, they need to know who you are. Add your organization name, mission statement, and a few key details. This takes about five minutes.

Go to **Settings > Organization Profile** to get started.

### Step 2: Pick One Thing to Try

Don't try to do everything at once. Pick one task you need done this week and take it to the right team member.

Good first tasks:
- Ask your **Development Director**: "What grants should we be applying for?"
- Ask your **Executive Assistant**: "Help me prep for tomorrow's board meeting."
- Ask your **Marketing Director**: "Write a social post about our latest program."

### Step 3: Review and Refine

Your team produces solid first drafts -- but you're the expert on your org. Read what they give you, make adjustments, and send it back for revisions if needed. The more you work with them, the better they get.

> **Try this now:** Open the chat with your Development Director and type: "What grants are a good fit for a [your mission area] nonprofit in [your city]?"

---

## What to Expect in Your First Week

**Day 1-2:** Get your org profile filled in. Try one or two conversations with different team members. Get a feel for how they talk and what they can do.

**Day 3-4:** Upload a few documents -- a past grant proposal, a program summary, your current budget. Your team will use these to give you better, more specific help.

**Day 5-7:** Start routing real work to your team. Have your Marketing Director draft your next newsletter. Ask Finance to build a simple budget template. Let your Executive Assistant organize your inbox.

By the end of the week, you'll know which team members you rely on most -- and you'll have a system that actually saves you time.

---

## A Few Things to Know

- **Your team remembers.** Every conversation is saved. Your team builds on what they learn about your org over time.
- **You're still in charge.** Your team produces drafts and recommendations. You review, approve, and decide.
- **They work fast, but they're not perfect.** Always read financial outputs carefully. Always review anything going out publicly. Think of your team as very capable interns who need your sign-off.
