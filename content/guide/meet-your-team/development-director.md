# Your Development Director

## Who They Are

Your Development Director is your fundraising engine. They're strategic, persistent, and deeply familiar with the grant landscape. They know how to speak to funders, how to steward donors, and how to build a case for your mission that actually gets funded.

They are methodical when they need to be and persuasive when they have to be. They treat every donor relationship like it matters -- because it does.

---

## What They're Great At

- Researching grants that match your mission and location
- Writing full grant proposals and letters of inquiry
- Drafting thank-you letters and donor acknowledgments
- Building donor stewardship strategies
- Updating your CRM with contact notes and next actions
- Creating fundraising reports for your board
- Reviewing funder guidelines and eligibility requirements
- Planning annual fund campaigns
- Drafting major gift asks

---

## When to Go to Them

Go to your Development Director when you need to:

- Find new funding opportunities you haven't considered
- Write or polish a grant application
- Follow up with a donor after a gift
- Build out your year-end appeal strategy
- Figure out why a donor lapsed and what to do about it
- Prepare a fundraising update for your board
- Track where you are against your revenue goals

---

## Example Prompts to Try

Copy and paste any of these to get started:

1. "What grants are a good fit for a youth mentoring nonprofit in [your city/state]?"
2. "Draft a thank-you letter for a $5,000 donation from a first-time donor."
3. "Write a letter of inquiry for the [funder name] grant. Our program focuses on [brief description]."
4. "Help me write the narrative section of a grant proposal for [program name]."
5. "A major donor hasn't given in 18 months. Draft a re-engagement email."
6. "Summarize our fundraising progress for the board. Here's the data: [paste your numbers]."
7. "What should our year-end appeal focus on this year?"
8. "Build a 12-month grant calendar based on these funders: [list funders]."
9. "Review this grant proposal and tell me what's weak: [paste text]."
10. "Draft a donor impact report for our spring gala attendees."

> **Try this now:** Paste this into the chat: "What are 5 grants we should apply for in the next 6 months? Our mission is [one sentence]."

---

## Tips for Working With Them

**Give them context.** The more they know about your org, the better the output. Include your program name, target population, location, and budget size when you can.

**Share your funder guidelines.** If you paste in a grant RFP or funder guidelines, they'll tailor the proposal to match exactly what the funder wants.

**Be specific about the donor.** "Draft a thank you letter" is fine. "Draft a thank you letter for Maria Chen, who has given for three years and cares about workforce development" is much better.

**Review all grant narratives carefully.** Your Development Director writes strong drafts -- but you know the real story of your work. Add the human details that only you have.
