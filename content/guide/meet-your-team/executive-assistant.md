# Your Executive Assistant

## Who They Are

Your Executive Assistant keeps everything moving. They handle the operational details that eat your day -- scheduling, email triage, meeting prep, and task tracking. They're calm, organized, and relentlessly on top of things.

They work best when you give them access to context: your calendar, your inbox, your current projects. The more they know about your schedule and priorities, the more they can take off your plate.

---

## What They're Great At

- Triaging and drafting email responses
- Scheduling and rescheduling meetings
- Building agendas and briefing notes for meetings
- Tracking action items from conversations and meetings
- Organizing your task list by priority
- Drafting internal communications and memos
- Preparing board meeting materials
- Sending follow-up reminders and summaries

---

## When to Go to Them

Go to your Executive Assistant when you need to:

- Clear your inbox and get on top of outstanding emails
- Prep for a meeting you haven't had time to think about
- Figure out what needs to happen next and by when
- Draft a quick internal update or memo
- Organize a messy to-do list into clear priorities
- Build an agenda for a team or board meeting
- Track commitments you've made to others

---

## Example Prompts to Try

Copy and paste any of these to get started:

1. "I have a board meeting on Thursday. Help me build an agenda. Topics: [list topics]."
2. "Triage these emails and tell me which need a response today: [paste subject lines + senders]."
3. "Draft a response to this email: [paste email]. Tone: professional but warm."
4. "Here's my messy to-do list. Organize it by priority: [paste list]."
5. "Write a memo to my staff about our new policy on [topic]."
6. "I met with [name] yesterday. They need [request]. Draft a follow-up email."
7. "Build a meeting agenda for a 45-minute strategy session with my leadership team."
8. "Summarize these action items from our last meeting: [paste notes]."
9. "I need to reschedule my 2pm meeting. Draft a polite message to move it to next week."
10. "What should I be focused on this week? Here's my current project list: [paste]."

> **Try this now:** "Here are my top 5 priorities this week. Help me plan Monday morning: [list your priorities]."

---

## Tips for Working With Them

**Give them the raw material.** Paste in your email threads, meeting notes, or to-do lists. They work best with real content to organize.

**Tell them your style.** Do you prefer bullet points or prose? Formal or casual? Brief or detailed? Setting your preference once saves time every interaction.

**Use them proactively.** Don't wait until you're overwhelmed. A 5-minute check-in at the start of the week keeps your task list clean and your schedule clear.

**They can't see your calendar directly** (unless you've connected it in Settings). But paste in your commitments and they'll help you plan around them.
