# Your Finance Director

## Who They Are

Your Finance Director is your numbers expert. They handle budgets, cash flow projections, grant financial reports, and audit preparation. They are precise, compliance-first, and always surfacing risks before they become problems.

They're calm under pressure, careful with details, and honest about what they don't know. They'll flag things that need a CPA's review rather than guess -- which is exactly what you want.

---

## What They're Great At

- Building and reviewing organizational and program budgets
- Forecasting cash flow and flagging potential shortfalls
- Writing the financial sections of grant reports
- Preparing for audits with checklists and documentation guidance
- Analyzing budget-to-actual variance
- Building financial dashboards for board meetings
- Reviewing grant spending against budget
- Explaining financial concepts in plain language

---

## When to Go to Them

Go to your Finance Director when you need to:

- Build a budget for a new program or grant application
- Understand where your cash flow stands for the next 90 days
- Prepare the financial section of a grant report
- Get ready for your annual audit
- Explain your finances to your board in plain terms
- Check whether you're on track against your budget
- Think through the financial implications of a major decision

---

## Example Prompts to Try

Copy and paste any of these to get started:

1. "Build a budget template for a 12-month youth mentoring program with a staff of 3."
2. "Help me forecast our cash position for the next 90 days. Here's our current balance and upcoming expenses: [paste data]."
3. "Write the financial narrative for our Q2 grant report. Budget: [X]. Spent: [Y]. Variance: [explain reasons]."
4. "What documents do I need to prepare for our annual audit?"
5. "Create a budget-to-actual variance report for our board. Here are the numbers: [paste]."
6. "We're thinking about hiring a new staff member. What does that actually cost, fully loaded?"
7. "Build a simple financial dashboard I can present at the next board meeting."
8. "This grant requires a 10% match. How do I document in-kind contributions?"
9. "Review this grant budget and flag any issues: [paste budget]."
10. "Explain our current financial position to our board in non-financial language."

> **Try this now:** "Help me build a basic operating budget for next fiscal year. Here's what we spent this year: [paste your expense categories and amounts]."

---

## Tips for Working With Them

**Always have them flag for professional review.** Your Finance Director can draft financial documents and catch issues, but tax filings, audit opinions, and complex accounting decisions need a licensed CPA. They'll remind you of this themselves.

**Give them actual numbers.** The more real data you provide, the more accurate their output. Round numbers for drafts, exact numbers for reports.

**Use them for board prep.** Translating financial statements into plain language for board members is one of their most valuable skills.

**Double-check anything going to funders.** Grant financial reports are formal documents. Review carefully before submission.
