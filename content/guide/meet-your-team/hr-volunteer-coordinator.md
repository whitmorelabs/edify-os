# Your HR & Volunteer Coordinator

## Who They Are

Your HR & Volunteer Coordinator is your people expert. They handle everything related to your staff and volunteers -- from hiring and onboarding to policies, training, and volunteer recognition. They're warm, inclusive, and naturally people-centered.

They make compliance approachable. They understand that HR isn't just about policies -- it's about building a workplace where people want to show up.

---

## What They're Great At

- Writing job descriptions and designing interview processes
- Building volunteer role descriptions and onboarding plans
- Drafting employee handbook sections and workplace policies
- Designing staff and volunteer training materials
- Creating volunteer recognition and retention programs
- Helping navigate performance conversations (with care)
- Building orientation schedules for new staff and volunteers
- Writing internal communications around HR topics

---

## When to Go to Them

Go to your HR & Volunteer Coordinator when you need to:

- Hire for a new role and don't know where to start
- Onboard a new staff member or volunteer cohort
- Write or update a policy (PTO, remote work, code of conduct)
- Design a volunteer program from scratch
- Figure out how to retain the volunteers you have
- Train your team on a new process or policy
- Draft a sensitive HR communication

---

## Example Prompts to Try

Copy and paste any of these to get started:

1. "Write a job description for a part-time Development Associate. Salary: $45,000."
2. "Design a one-week onboarding plan for a new program coordinator."
3. "Draft a volunteer recruitment post for our summer literacy program."
4. "Write a remote work policy for our 5-person staff."
5. "Build an onboarding checklist for new volunteers."
6. "Create a volunteer appreciation email for our annual recognition week."
7. "Design interview questions for a marketing coordinator role."
8. "Help me write a performance review template for our program staff."
9. "Draft talking points for a difficult conversation with a staff member about [issue]."
10. "What should our volunteer onboarding include for a food pantry setting?"

> **Try this now:** "Write a job description for a [role name] at a nonprofit serving [population]. Pay range: [salary range]."

---

## Tips for Working With Them

**Always note your jurisdiction.** Employment law varies by state. Your HR Coordinator will flag when something needs review by an employment attorney in your area.

**Be clear about your org size.** HR obligations differ for orgs with under 15 employees, 50 employees, etc. Tell them your headcount when it's relevant.

**They're a draft-starter, not a lawyer.** For terminations, discrimination claims, or formal disciplinary actions, always involve qualified HR counsel.

**Use them to build culture, not just compliance.** They're excellent at drafting recognition programs, team communications, and onboarding experiences that make people feel welcome.
