# Your Marketing Director

## Who They Are

Your Marketing Director is the voice of your organization. They're creative, strategic, and always thinking about your audience. They know how to match your message to the right platform, write copy that actually gets read, and build a consistent brand -- even when you're stretched thin.

They're energetic and detail-oriented. They care about what you say and how you say it.

---

## What They're Great At

- Writing social media posts for LinkedIn, Instagram, Facebook, and X
- Drafting email newsletters and campaigns
- Writing blog posts, press releases, and case studies
- Building content calendars and messaging frameworks
- Designing communication plans for campaigns or announcements
- Reviewing and improving existing content
- Analyzing campaign performance and suggesting improvements
- Writing talking points and media pitches
- Developing brand voice guidelines

---

## When to Go to Them

Go to your Marketing Director when you need to:

- Post something on social media and want it to land right
- Draft your monthly newsletter or an email to your list
- Announce a new program, event, or milestone
- Figure out what to post and when (content calendar)
- Write a press release for local media
- Polish language before it goes out publicly
- Understand whether your campaigns are working

---

## Example Prompts to Try

Copy and paste any of these to get started:

1. "Write three LinkedIn posts about our new mentorship program launching next month."
2. "Draft an email newsletter for March. Topics: [list topics]. Tone: warm and community-focused."
3. "Write a press release announcing our partnership with [organization name]."
4. "Build a 30-day content calendar for Instagram. We focus on [your mission area]."
5. "Rewrite this paragraph so it's warmer and less formal: [paste text]."
6. "Write an Instagram caption for this photo of our volunteers at [event]."
7. "What subject lines would get more opens for our year-end appeal emails?"
8. "Draft a case study about one of our program graduates. Here are the details: [paste]."
9. "Create a messaging framework for our capital campaign announcement."
10. "Analyze these email stats and tell me what to do differently: [paste open rates, click rates, etc.]."

> **Try this now:** "Write three social media posts announcing our upcoming [event or program]. Keep them warm and community-focused. One for LinkedIn, one for Instagram, one for Facebook."

---

## Tips for Working With Them

**Tell them your audience.** "Write a post" and "write a post for longtime volunteers who've been with us 5+ years" will give you very different results. Audience context matters.

**Share your brand voice.** If you have a style guide or examples of content you love, paste them in. Your Director will match your tone.

**Give them a platform.** Instagram posts, LinkedIn articles, and email newsletters have different rules. Always say which platform you're writing for.

**Review before publishing.** Your Marketing Director writes good drafts fast -- but you know your community. Read it like your audience would before hitting send.
