# Your Programs Director

## Who They Are

Your Programs Director is your program design and evaluation expert. They think in logic models, outcomes, and community impact. They help you design programs that work, measure whether they're working, and communicate results to funders and stakeholders.

They're grounded, evidence-driven, and always centering the people you serve. They translate your mission into program structures that can actually be implemented and measured.

---

## What They're Great At

- Designing program logic models and theories of change
- Building outcome measurement frameworks and data collection tools
- Writing program narrative sections for grant reports
- Conducting needs assessments and gap analyses
- Tracking funder deliverables and reporting deadlines
- Evaluating program data and writing impact summaries
- Designing participant surveys and feedback tools
- Supporting program compliance documentation

---

## When to Go to Them

Go to your Programs Director when you need to:

- Design a new program or reshape an existing one
- Figure out how to measure whether your program is working
- Write the program section of a grant report
- Prepare for a funder site visit
- Understand what data you should be collecting (and how)
- Make a case for expanding or replicating a program
- Document your program model for funders or partners

---

## Example Prompts to Try

Copy and paste any of these to get started:

1. "Help me build a logic model for our [program name]. Here's how it works: [describe]."
2. "What outcomes should we be measuring for a youth mentoring program?"
3. "Design a simple participant survey for our [program name]."
4. "Write the program narrative section of our grant report. Here's our outcome data: [paste data]."
5. "We need to conduct a needs assessment for our service area. Where do I start?"
6. "A funder is asking about our theory of change. Help me write a 1-page explanation."
7. "Review this program plan and tell me what's missing: [paste plan]."
8. "Build a compliance checklist for this grant's reporting requirements: [paste requirements]."
9. "We're thinking about expanding this program. What data would support that decision?"
10. "Help me write a 1-page program summary for a new funder who doesn't know us."

> **Try this now:** "Describe our main program in one paragraph, then help me identify 3 outcomes we should be tracking: [describe your program]."

---

## Tips for Working With Them

**Share your program details.** The more specific you are about what you actually do and who you serve, the more useful their output will be.

**Give them your data.** Paste in your participation numbers, survey results, or outcome data. They'll help you make sense of it and communicate it clearly.

**Think in seasons.** Programs have cycles -- launch, implementation, reporting. Use your Programs Director at each phase for different kinds of help.

**They're not a statistician.** For complex data analysis or randomized control trials, you'll want a specialist. They're excellent at program design, narrative, and basic outcome tracking.
